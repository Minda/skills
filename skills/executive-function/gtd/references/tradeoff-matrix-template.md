## Common Patterns to Consider

### Ship + Apply Pattern
**Focus:** External-facing deliverables (job apps, portfolio shipping)
**Best when:** Career transition is time-sensitive, have good portfolio pieces ready
**Risk:** Internal infrastructure degrades, learning pauses

### Build + Learn Pattern
**Focus:** Deep work on projects + skill development
**Best when:** Building portfolio, early in learning curve, no urgent deadlines
**Risk:** No external progress, opportunity costs accumulate

### Hybrid Pattern
**Focus:** Balance multiple areas with time-boxing
**Best when:** Multiple important goals, good at context switching, realistic about capacity
**Risk:** Nothing reaches completion, overhead from switching

### Infrastructure + Reflection Pattern
**Focus:** Meta-work, systems building, planning
**Best when:** Actually needed (new workflow, broken systems), Sunday reflection
**Risk:** Productive procrastination, avoiding real work

## Decision Criteria to Evaluate

Common dimensions for the decision matrix:

1. **Career Progress** - External outcomes (applications, interviews, offers)
2. **Portfolio Quality** - Demonstrable work for future opportunities
3. **Learning Depth** - Skill development and knowledge acquisition
4. **Momentum Feel** - Psychological satisfaction and energy
5. **Deadline Pressure** - Time-sensitivity of various options
6. **Leverage** - Long-term impact vs. short-term gain
7. **Risk Mitigation** - Hedging against different failure modes
8. **Energy Match** - Alignment with current energy/capacity

## How to Use This Template

### Step 1: List Your Options (3-5 allocation patterns)

For each option, specify:
- Major activities (2-4 per option)
- Hours allocated to each
- Visual bar chart representation

### Step 2: Identify Tradeoffs

For each option, list:
- ❌ What you explicitly give up (3-5 items)
- ✅ What you explicitly gain (3-5 items)
- ⚠️ Risks or uncertainties

### Step 3: Create Decision Matrix

Score each option (0-8 blocks) on 3-5 key criteria:
- ████████ = Perfect match (8/8)
- ██████░░ = Good match (6/8)
- ████░░░░ = Okay match (4/8)
- ██░░░░░░ = Poor match (2/8)
- ░░░░░░░░ = Terrible match (0/8)

### Step 4: Calculate Hidden Costs

**Context Switching Tax:**
- Low: Single focus area or closely related work
- Medium: 2-3 different areas requiring different mindsets
- High: 4+ areas or very different types of work

**Opportunity Costs:**
- What compounds each week you delay?
- What windows of opportunity close?
- What momentum gets lost?

### Step 5: Apply Anti-Sycophancy Check

**State the strongest objection** to your preferred option:
- What's the best argument against this choice?
- What am I avoiding by choosing this?
- What pattern am I repeating?
- What would someone who knows me well say?

**Future Self Test:**
- Will you-in-1-month thank you-now?
- Will you-in-6-months thank you-now?
- What would you-from-last-week say?

## Integration with Planning

This tradeoff matrix should be created:
- **Weekly planning** (Sunday evening or Monday morning)
- **Mid-week check-in** (Wednesday - are tradeoffs playing out as expected?)
- **When priorities shift** (new deadline, opportunity, or blocker)
- **Quarterly review** (look at pattern of choices over time)

The matrix helps surface:
1. **Recurring patterns** (are you always choosing infrastructure over shipping?)
2. **Hidden assumptions** (why do you keep avoiding X?)
3. **Real costs** (what's the actual price of your choices?)
4. **Better options** (combinations you hadn't considered)
